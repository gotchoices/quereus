The `'join-residual'` arm — a fourth row-time maintenance strategy for **1:1 row-preserving
inner/cross joins** (`select … from T join P on T.fk = P.id`, exactly one MV row per governed
T row) — shipped. It reuses the residual kernel from the aggregate arm (`3.2`) on the driving
(`T`) side and adds a reverse (`P`-keyed) upsert-only residual for the lookup side. The join
soundness predicates were factored out of `coverage-prover.ts` into shared exported helpers.

See the now-current `docs/materialized-views.md` § `'join-residual'` and
`docs/incremental-maintenance.md` § join-residual for the mechanism, binding, and the
lookup-side soundness argument.

### Aspect scrutiny
- **Lookup-side upsert-only soundness (the crux) — VERIFIED SOUND.** The no-delete reverse
  residual is correct because the set `{T : T.fk = P.pk}` is fixed by `T.fk` (a `T` column a
  `P` write cannot touch) for an inner/cross join with **enforced** RI and no
  lookup-referencing WHERE. The decisive premise — that RI is *enforced*, not merely declared —
  is confirmed empirically: the equivalence harness's `execTolerant` swallows only
  `StatusCode.CONSTRAINT`, and the property test relies on a `p`-delete with referencing
  children surfacing as a (tolerated) CONSTRAINT error. So a `P` delete / PK-change that would
  orphan a backing row is *blocked by RI* and can never reach maintenance; a `P` insert/delete
  of an unreferenced row yields an empty residual (no-op). Both unsound shapes (outer join,
  WHERE-on-join) are rejected at build — and those rejections now have tests (below).
- **Routing / diagnostic ordering — correct.** `buildMaintenancePlan` checks
  SetOperation/Window/DISTINCT/RecursiveCTE/LIMIT *before* the join routing, and the
  `tableRefs.length > 1 && !containsAnyJoin` guard keeps a 2-table UNION and a `where … in
  (subquery)` body on the source-count tail (their subquery refs are collected but not
  decorrelated to a join node in `optimizeForAnalysis`). Verified by the passing
  `materialized-view-diagnostics.spec.ts` union / IN-subquery cases.
- **Forward (`T`) path — correct** across insert / FK-update / PK-change / delete (size-1
  group reuse of `applyForwardResidual`; key-changing update recomputes both OLD and NEW key).
- **`coverage-prover` refactor — no regression.** The descent was extracted verbatim into
  `walkToConstrainedBase`; `proveCoverage` kept its own inline fan-out gate so its diagnostic
  ordering is byte-for-byte unchanged; the full coverage-prover/sqllogic suites stayed green.
- SPP / DRY / modular / maintainable / type-safety / resource-cleanup / error-handling: the
