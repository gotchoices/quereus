## What landed

`docs/optimizer-fd.md` — a standalone document holding what used to be
`### Functional Dependency Tracking`, a 10,154-word section nested under the
unrelated `## Visited Tracking Architecture` heading in `docs/optimizer.md`. Every
`####` child was promoted to `##`, the one `#####` to `###`. `docs/optimizer.md`
kept a two-sentence stub plus a link where the section used to be, and shrank from
37,997 to 27,929 words.

The document covers: the `FunctionalDependency` / `GuardPredicate` /
`InclusionDependency` type surface, the `kind` uniqueness-provenance field and its
construction-site table, the collation gate on equality facts, the reader rule
(`isUniqueDeterminant`) and the `keysOf` / `isUnique` read path, equivalence
classes, constant bindings, the per-operator FD/EC propagation table, inclusion
dependencies, check-derived contributions, guarded FDs, coverage proving,
assertion-derived premises, guard activation, the `fd-utils.ts` helper surface,
singleton equivalence, consumers, and a `## Rejected alternatives` section.

### Editorial pass

Narrative history was deleted rather than archived, per `docs/doc-conventions.md`.
Removed: eleven ticket slugs cited as if they were section names; the "Wave 1 /
Wave 2 / Wave 3" roadmap labels on the inclusion-dependency subsystem; "Phase 2 …
removed every producer-side single↔single drop gate"; "renamed from `isSuperkey`";
"replaces the former `dropSideKeyFds`"; "used to be `uniqueKeys: [[]]`"; "the old
proper-subset guard is gone"; "the former `isAssertedKey` is subsumed"; "the prior
hardcoded `keys: [[]]` over-claimed".

Each of those that carried a *why-not* was condensed into `## Rejected alternatives`
(seven bullets: producer-side drop gates, a separate `uniqueKeys` field, an optional
`kind` field, the `isSuperkey` name, wholesale key-FD drops on fan-out, the
`isUnique` proper-subset guard, transitive IND closure).

### Considered and deliberately not changed

- **`## Rejected alternatives` vs. `doc-conventions.md`'s `### Rejected alternatives`.**
  The convention names an h3. Every topic doc in `docs/` uses `#` for its title and
  `##` for its sections, so an h3 would nest the section under whatever `##` precedes
  it. `##` is the right analogue and the checker does not enforce the level. If the
  convention meant a literal h3, the convention is what needs fixing, not this doc.
- **`fdsEqual`'s "ignores `kind`, `source`, and `valueEquality`" appears twice** (once
  under `## \`kind\`: uniqueness provenance`, once under `## Helper surface`). The
  implementer called it deliberate redundancy. Agreed — the two readers arrive from
  different directions and either one missing it is a soundness trap. It becomes a
  single entry in `docs/invariants.md` when `docs-invariants-optimizer` lands.
- **`docs/optimizer.md` still cites `tickets/complete/…` paths in the access-path rule
  prose, and the FD stub still sits under `## Visited Tracking Architecture`.** Both
  are outside the FD subtree; re-homing the eight misfiled sections is
  `docs-optimizer-split-satellites`, and the remaining slug citations belong to
  `debt-docs-shrink-remaining-megadocs`.
