# Complete: store-and-forward outbound relay

## What landed

The **outbound half** of the `store-and-forward` unknown-table disposition. The
prereq (`sync-store-and-forward-hold`) made apply durably **hold** a straggler's
out-of-basis change marked *forwardable*; this ticket makes those held changes
actually **reach** peers that still hold the table by folding them into this peer's
outbound `getChangesSince` → `ChangeSet[]` return — **no new transport surface**
(client/coordinator untouched).

- `collectForwardableChanges(peerSiteId, sinceHLC?)` — scans
  `quarantine.listForwardable()`, drops echoes (`change.hlc.siteId === peerSiteId`),
  and on the delta path keeps only `compareHLC(change.hlc, sinceHLC) > 0`.
- `getChangesSince` merges `[...dataChanges, ...forwardable]` (spread allocated only
  when non-empty) before `buildTransactionChangeSets`, which re-groups by
  `deterministicTxnId` and re-bounds the union at `batchSize` on a transaction
  boundary. Forwarded changes keep their **original `hlc` + `siteId`** — the identity
  that makes the relay convergent and loop-free with no per-table peer-membership
  oracle.
- `relayed` cumulative counter surfaced in `getUnknownTableStats()` (shape now
  `{ ignored, quarantined, forwarded, relayed, byTable }`) and `manager.ts`.
- Docs (`sync.md` disposition table + stats + new § Store-and-forward relay;
  `migration.md` § 4 + § Current gaps) brought to the both-parts-implemented state.

## Review findings

Adversarial pass over implement commit `6c8bce91`. Read the full diff (source +
tests + docs) before the handoff summary.

### Verified — correct

- **Load-bearing watermark invariant.** Forwarded changes keep their original HLC;
  the consumer advances `lastSyncHLC = max(ChangeSet.hlc)`. The `> sinceHLC` filter
  in `collectForwardableChanges` is what prevents a forwarded-only round from
  regressing the watermark. Confirmed against the change-log contract and exercised
  by the below-watermark and re-relay specs.
- **Bound + ordering under truncation.** Traced the merge → `groupByTransaction`
  (ascending base-HLC) → `batchSize` cut in `change-grouping.ts`. The merged response
  is a contiguous HLC-ordered prefix: everything ≤ the cut `M` is present from both
  the (early-exiting) change-log scan and the (full) forwardable scan, and forwardable
  entries beyond `M` are re-collected next round (still `> sinceHLC`). The
  implementer flagged the absence of a forced-truncation test as the highest-value
  gap — **fixed inline** (see Fixed below).
- **No double-delivery.** A change is either applied to a known table (→ change log)
  or diverted to quarantine — mutually exclusive — so the union needs no dedup.
- **Mixed live+retired transaction.** A straggler txn writing both a live (→ change
  log) and a retired-here (→ forwardable) table rejoins into one ChangeSet by
  `deterministicTxnId` (same base HLC). Correct by construction; consistent
  echo-exclusion on both sources.
- **Echo exclusion, from-zero path, GC interaction, telemetry.** All covered by the
  existing 9 specs; re-read and confirmed they assert what they claim.
- **Coordinator / relay-only path.** With no basis oracle, detection is inert →
  nothing is ever held forwardable → `collectForwardableChanges` returns `[]`
  (an empty, horizon-bounded `qt:` scan per `getChangesSince`). Cheap, correct.
- **Type safety / shape consistency.** `getUnknownTableStats` shape is declared only
  in `manager.ts` and implemented in `sync-manager-impl.ts`, both updated; the
  sync-client `MockSyncManager` updated. No other shape declarations
  (`protocol.ts` / `sync-context.ts` hits are comments only). `siteIdEquals` /
  `compareHLC` already imported.

### Fixed inline (minor)

- **Added a forced-truncation bound test** (`store-and-forward-relay.spec.ts`, +1 →
  10 specs): `batchSize: 1` over a union of two live change-log writes (HLC 1000,
  2000) straddling a forwarded change (1500). Pulls round-by-round advancing the
  watermark to `max(ChangeSet.hlc)`; asserts all three are delivered exactly once in
  global HLC order (forwarded change interleaved at 1500), the deferred forwardable
  tail is re-collected the next round, and the stream drains with no gap or
  duplicate. Required a `batchSize` option on the spec's `makePeer` helper. This
  closes the implementer's stated highest-value gap.

### Judged acceptable — no change

- **`relayed` over-counts under truncation.** Bumped by candidate count at collection
  time, before `buildTransactionChangeSets` may defer some past the batch bound
  (re-counted next round). It is observe-only telemetry, explicitly documented as
  "relay activity, not distinct deliveries." Not misleading given the documented
  semantics; left as-is (it was the ticket's optional item).
- **Watermark is a test variable, not durable peer-state, in the specs.** The relay
  logic only reads the `sinceHLC` it is passed; how the watermark is persisted
  (`updatePeerSyncState` / `peerStates`) is orthogonal and covered elsewhere in the
  suite. Faithful to the transport contract.
