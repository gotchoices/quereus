## Substantive doc claims I found wrong and did NOT fix

The ticket instructed that a wrong claim is a finding, not a scope extension. These are the
ones I saw and left:

1. **`ApplyNode` does not exist.** `docs/optimizer-retrieve.md § ApplyNode Architecture for
   Correlated Joins` (~1,300 words) describes an `ApplyNode` abstraction that "replaces
   traditional `JoinNode` semantics". There is no `ApplyNode` anywhere under
   `packages/quereus/src` — correlated and lateral joins plan as `JoinNode`s today. I added
   a three-line `> **Status: design proposal, not implemented.**` banner at the top of the
   section and left the body untouched. Per `docs/doc-conventions.md` this is future work
   and should leave the docs entirely (→ `docs/todo.md`), but that is a ~1,300-word deletion
   whose substance a reviewer should sign off on, not a mechanical split. **This is the
   single biggest open decision in this ticket.**
2. **QuickPick's `### Algorithm Design` code block is not the implementation.** It shows a
   `class QuickPickOptimizer` with `async optimizeJoins()` and a `Math.random()` shuffle.
   The real rule is `rules/join/rule-quickpick-enumeration.ts`, a plain
   `(node, context) => PlanNode | null` rule with an `extractJoinGraph` front end. The block
   reads as an illustrative sketch; I left it. Its neighbouring `### Integration Points`
   *is* accurate (Physical pass, priority 5).
3. **`estimatedCostFromNode.getTotalCost()`** is cited in QuickPick's `### Integration
   Points`. I did not find that symbol; `PlanNode.getTotalCost()` is the real surface.
   Possibly a mangled sentence rather than a dead symbol, so I left it.
4. **`## Optimization Pipeline Architecture` (hub) phases 3–4** describe join enumeration by
   "traditional dynamic programming" and a "cost-precise" advanced-pushdown phase. Neither
   is a real pass; QuickPick is randomized greedy, not DP. The material duplicates
   `docs/todo.md § Push-down & Federation Roadmap`. Left in the hub, unmodified.
